Rolling Churn (3-Month, Weighted)

\text{Rolling Churn (\%)} =
\left(
\frac{
\sum_{i=1}^{3} \text{Cancels}_i
}{
\sum_{i=1}^{3} \text{Active Members at Start}_i
}
\right)
\times 100

⸻

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)

\text{MRR} =
\sum (\text{Monthly-Equivalent Rate per Active Member})

Monthly-equivalent conversions:

\text{Annual Rate} = \frac{\text{Annual Price}}{12}

\text{Weekly Rate} = \frac{\text{Weekly Price} \times 52}{12}

⸻

ARM (Average Revenue per Member)

\text{ARM} = \frac{\text{MRR}}{\text{Total Active Members}}

⸻

LTV (Lifetime Value)

Use rolling churn.

\text{Average Lifespan (months)} =
\frac{1}{\text{Rolling Churn as Decimal}}

\text{LTV} = \text{ARM} \times \text{Average Lifespan}

If Rolling Churn ≤ 0.5%:

\text{Average Lifespan} = 20 \text{ months (cap)}

⸻

LTVE Impact (Annual Revenue Gain from 1% Churn Reduction)

Let:

\Delta C = 0.01

\text{Annual Gain} =
\text{Active Members}
\times \Delta C
\times \text{ARM}
\times 12

RSI (Retention Stability Index)

\text{RSI Raw} =
100
- P_{\text{churn}}
- P_{\text{early}}
+ B_{\text{tenure}}
+ B_{\text{growth}}

\text{RSI} = \min(100,\max(0,\text{RSI Raw}))

⸻

RES (Revenue Efficiency Score)

\text{RES} =
\text{ARM Tier Points}
+
\text{Volume Tier Points}
+
\text{MRR Tier Points}

\text{RES} = \min(100,\text{RES})

Member Risk Probability

Step 1 — Risk Score

S =
S_{\text{tenure}}
+
S_{\text{outreach}}
+
S_{\text{gym churn}}
+
S_{\text{rate}}
+
S_{\text{urgency}}

⸻

Step 2 — Logistic Mapping to Probability

p =
\frac{1}{1 + e^{-(a + bS)}}

Where:
	•	a = -3.5
	•	b = 0.15

⸻

Step 3 — Clamp Output

\text{Risk Probability} =
\min(0.95,\max(0.01,p))

Expected Revenue Impact

\text{Impact} =
\text{Members Affected}
\times
\text{ARM}
\times
\text{Months Horizon}
\times
\text{Estimated Lift (decimal)}
\times
\text{Urgency Multiplier}

⸻

Intervention Score

Convert confidence to decimal:

C_d = \frac{\text{Confidence}}{100}

\text{Intervention Score} =
\text{Impact}
\times
C_d
\times
\text{Urgency}

⸻

Confidence Normalization

\text{Confidence} =
\min(100,\text{Sum of Confidence Components})
